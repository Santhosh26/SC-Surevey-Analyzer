# QUICK WINS IDENTIFICATION GUIDE
## Extracting Immediate Value from Survey Data

**Purpose:** Transform Q13 (Stop Doing) and Q14 (Start Doing) responses into prioritized action items that can be executed within 30-90 days.

---

## WHY QUICK WINS MATTER

### 1. Momentum
- Demonstrates leadership is listening and acting
- Builds credibility for longer-term transformation
- Creates energy and buy-in for bigger changes

### 2. Capacity Creation
- Stop Doing = Free up time/resources
- Start Doing = Invest freed capacity strategically
- Creates space for medium/long-term initiatives

### 3. Trust Building
- Shows survey wasn't just a checkbox exercise
- Validates that team input drives decisions
- Encourages future participation and honesty

---

## PHASE 1: DATA EXTRACTION

### Step 1: Export and Clean

**From Q13 (Stop Doing):**
1. Copy all responses to a separate sheet
2. Remove duplicates (exactly identical text)
3. Clean obvious typos but preserve meaning
4. Remove non-responses ("N/A", "Nothing", "Not sure")

**From Q14 (Start Doing):**
1. Same process as Q13
2. Separate "start doing" from "do differently" if needed

**Expected volume:** 80-100 unique ideas per question (from ~100 respondents)

---

### Step 2: Initial Categorization

**Read through all responses and create 8-10 broad categories:**

**Common Categories for Q13 (Stop Doing):**
- Meetings/Calls (unnecessary syncs, status updates)
- Manual/Admin Work (data entry, reporting)
- Redundant Processes (duplicate approvals, forms)
- Low-Value Activities (non-core work)
- Tool/System Issues (using broken/outdated tools)
- Communication Overhead (excessive emails, Slack)

**Common Categories for Q14 (Start Doing):**
- Training/Development (skills, tools, processes)
- Tools/Technology (new systems, automation)
- Knowledge Sharing (documentation, best practices)
- Process Improvements (streamline workflows)
- Cross-functional Collaboration (better coordination)
- Customer Focus (more strategic engagement)

**Tag each response with 1-2 categories**

## PHASE 3: IMPACT ASSESSMENT

### Step 5: Estimate Impact

For each item, rate impact on **3 dimensions:**

#### 5.1 Time Savings (for Stop items)
- **High (3 pts):** Saves >5 hours per person per week
- **Medium (2 pts):** Saves 2-5 hours per person per week
- **Low (1 pt):** Saves <2 hours per person per week

#### 5.2 Value Creation (for Start items)
- **High (3 pts):** Directly increases win rate, customer satisfaction, or revenue
- **Medium (2 pts):** Improves team effectiveness or collaboration
- **Low (1 pt):** Nice to have but not game-changing

#### 5.3 Scope of Impact
- **High (3 pts):** Affects >75% of team
- **Medium (2 pts):** Affects 40-75% of team
- **Low (1 pt):** Affects <40% of team

**Calculate Impact Score:** Average of 3 dimensions (1-3 scale)

**Example:**

| Stop Item | Time Savings | Value | Scope | Impact Score |
|-----------|--------------|-------|-------|--------------|
| Reduce meetings | High (3) | Medium (2) | High (3) | 2.7 |
| Automate reports | High (3) | Medium (2) | Medium (2) | 2.3 |
| Stop manual CRM entry | Medium (2) | Low (1) | High (3) | 2.0 |

## PHASE 4: EFFORT ESTIMATION

### Step 6: Estimate Implementation Effort

**Use T-shirt sizing:**

#### Low Effort (1 week or less)
- Leadership decision/announcement
- Policy change
- Stopping an existing practice
- Simple tool adoption

**Examples:**
- "Cancel recurring status meeting"
- "Stop requiring triple approval for demos"
- "Start using ChatGPT for RFP responses"

---

#### Medium Effort (2-8 weeks)
- Process redesign
- Tool procurement + setup
- Training program development
- Cross-team coordination

**Examples:**
- "Implement automated reporting dashboard"
- "Launch AI tool training series"
- "Create shared knowledge base"

---

#### High Effort (>8 weeks)
- System integration
- Major org change
- Custom development
- Require significant budget/resources

**Examples:**
- "Build automated demo environment provisioning"
- "Implement new CRM with AI"
- "Restructure presales organization"

**Quick Wins = Low Effort only**

**Priority Definitions:**

**P1 (Quick Wins):** High Impact + Low Effort
- **Timeline:** 0-30 days
- **Approval:** Minimal (director level)
- **Resources:** Existing budget/capacity
- **Communication:** Announce immediately

**P2 (Strategic Projects):** High Impact + High Effort
- **Timeline:** 60-180 days
- **Approval:** VP/C-level
- **Resources:** Budget allocation required
- **Communication:** Roadmap item

**P3 (Consider):** Low Impact + Low Effort
- **Timeline:** 30-90 days
- **Approval:** Team lead
- **Resources:** Spare capacity
- **Communication:** Optional

**P4 (Defer):** Low Impact + High Effort
- **Timeline:** Backlog
- **Approval:** N/A
- **Resources:** N/A
- **Communication:** Politely decline

## PITFALLS TO AVOID

### 1. Analysis Paralysis
**Problem:** Spending weeks perfecting the analysis
**Solution:** 80/20 rule - identify top 5 quick wins and execute

### 2. Scope Creep
**Problem:** Quick wins turn into major projects
**Solution:** If it takes >1 week, it's not a quick win â†’ medium-term roadmap

### 3. No Follow-Through
**Problem:** Announce with fanfare, never implement
**Solution:** Assign clear owners and deadlines; track publicly

### 4. No Measurement
**Problem:** Can't prove impact
**Solution:** Define 1-2 metrics upfront; measure before/after

### 5. Solving for the Vocal Minority
**Problem:** Focusing on 1-2 complaints, not 30+ mentions
**Solution:** Use frequency data; validate with broader team

### 6. Forgetting to Close the Loop
**Problem:** Team doesn't know action was taken based on their input
**Solution:** Always connect quick wins back to survey: "You said X, we did Y"
